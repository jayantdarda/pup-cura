Daily Care

Training Guide

Basic Commands

  • Sit
  • Stay
  • Come
  • Heel

Positive Reinforcement

Reward good behavior with treats, praise, or play to build trust and encourage learning.

Socialization Tips

Expose your dog to different environments, people, and animals early to build confidence.

Common Training Mistakes

Avoid inconsistent commands, punishment, and skipping short daily sessions.

Diet & Nutrition

Recommended Food Types

High-quality proteins, healthy grains, and vet-approved formulas tailored to your dog's age and size.

Healthy Treats

Opt for natural, low-calorie treats like carrots, apples, or specially formulated dental chews.

Foods To Avoid

Keep chocolate, grapes, onions, and xylitol products far away from your furry friend.

Hydration Importance

Ensure fresh water is always available to support digestion, temperature regulation, and overall health.

Feeding Schedule

Consistent meal times help regulate metabolism and prevent anxiety. Typically, adult dogs do well with two meals a day.

Health & Wellness

Vaccination Awareness

Stay up-to-date with core vaccines to protect against rabies, distemper, and parvovirus.

Routine Vet Visits

Annual checkups catch potential issues early and keep your dog's health on track.

Dental Care

Brush regularly and provide dental chews to prevent gum disease and bad breath.

Exercise Importance

Daily walks and play keep joints healthy, weight in check, and minds stimulated.

Signs Of Illness

Watch for changes in appetite, energy, breathing, or behavior, and consult your vet promptly.

Daily Care Tips

Grooming

Regular brushing reduces shedding and keeps the coat healthy and shiny.

Bathing

Use dog-friendly shampoo and bathe as needed based on coat type and activity level.

Walking

Daily walks provide exercise, mental stimulation, and bonding time.

Mental Stimulation

Puzzle toys and training games keep your dog's mind sharp and engaged.

Sleep Requirements

Adult dogs need 12-14 hours of sleep daily. Provide a quiet, cozy resting spot.
